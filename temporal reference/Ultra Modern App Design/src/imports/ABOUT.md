# Companion

**Thoughtful messages, beautifully written.**

---

## What is Companion?

Companion helps you write warm, genuine messages for someone you love — the kind of note you'd send on WhatsApp after thinking about them for a moment, not the kind you'd find on a greeting card.

You open the app, generate a short message, read it, copy it, and paste it into WhatsApp yourself. That's the whole workflow. Companion doesn't send anything for you. It doesn't connect to WhatsApp, schedule messages, or automate anything. It's a writing companion: a quiet place to craft something meaningful before you share it by hand.

---

## Who is it for?

Companion is for anyone who cares deeply about someone and wants their daily messages to feel personal — not repetitive, not generic, not copied from the internet.

Maybe you live apart during the week. Maybe you want to say something real but you're tired at the end of the day. Maybe you know what you feel but struggle to put it into words. Companion gives you a starting point that sounds like *you*, shaped by what you know about the person you're writing to.

---

## What does it do?

### 1. Learns who you're writing for

The first time you use Companion, it asks two simple things:

- **Who you're writing for** — Her or Him
- **A little about them** — whatever helps the messages feel personal: how long you've been together, what they love, what to avoid, anything that matters

This stays on your device and quietly informs every message from then on. You can change it anytime in Settings.

### 2. Writes with today's world in mind

When you generate a message, Companion considers the moment you're in:

- The day and time of day
- The weather in Accra
- Themes you've used recently (so it doesn't repeat itself)

The result should feel timely and fresh — like something you'd actually say today, not a template you sent last Tuesday.

### 3. Gives you one message at a time

Each generation produces a single short message — no more than a few sentences. Warm, human, specific. Not a wall of text. Not a poem unless the tone calls for it.

Along with the message, you'll see a **theme** (like gratitude or tenderness) and a **tone** (like warm or affectionate) — subtle labels that describe the spirit of what was written.

### 4. Lets you copy and go

When a message feels right, you tap **Copy**, paste it into WhatsApp, and send it yourself. Only then is it saved to your history — so your list stays full of messages you actually sent, not every draft you considered.

If it's not quite right, tap **Regenerate** for something new.

## How it looks and feels

Companion is designed to feel **premium, calm, and editorial** — closer to a luxury magazine or a high-end property listing than a typical app. Warm stone backgrounds. Bronze accents. Lots of breathing room. Nothing loud, nothing pink, nothing that screams "romance app."

### The landing moment

When you arrive, you're greeted with a large, confident headline:

**Write What  
You Truly Feel.**

Below that, a quiet line: *Thoughtful messages crafted for the people who matter most.*

On desktop, the screen splits in two — words on the left, a warm editorial photograph on the right: sunlight, a journal, the feeling of someone pausing to write a letter. On mobile, the same elements stack gently: headline, button, then image.

A single bronze button invites you forward: **Generate Message**.

### After you generate

The photograph gives way to your message. It sits on the page like editorial copy in a magazine — no heavy box around it, no dashboard chrome. Large serif typography. Generous line spacing. The words are the hero.

Above the message, a small glass capsule shows the weather — something like *☁ Rainy in Accra • 28°C · Thursday* — subtle context, not a weather report.

Thin horizontal lines divide sections the way a well-designed editorial page would. Theme and tone appear quietly beneath the message. **Copy** and **Regenerate** sit below as understated outline buttons.

### History

Tap **Recent Messages →** and a panel slides in from the right — full height, clean white surface, dates and message previews listed like entries in a personal journal. No dropdowns. No clutter.

### Settings

A simple overlay lets you update who you're writing for and the personal context you've shared. Same calm form as the first-time setup.

### Overall mood

- Warm porcelain background (`#F5F1EA`)
- Deep charcoal text
- Bronze accents
- Cormorant Garamond for headlines and messages
- Inter for buttons and labels
- Soft glow in the corner, barely-there grain across the page
- Glass-effect weather capsule

The feeling: *you're crafting something meaningful, not filling out a form.*

## A typical session

1. Open Companion in your browser
2. If it's your first visit, tell it who you're writing for and a little about them
3. Read the headline, take a breath
4. Tap **Generate Message**
5. Wait a few seconds while it writes
6. Read the message on the right (or below, on mobile)
7. Like it? **Copy** → paste into WhatsApp → send
8. Want another angle? **Regenerate**
9. Later, check **Recent Messages** to see what you've sent before

That's it. No accounts. No notifications. No integrations. Just you, a moment of thought, and a message worth sending.

---

## What Companion is not

- **Not a messaging app** — it never sends anything
- **Not a chatbot** — you don't have a conversation with it; it drafts one message for you
- **Not a social platform** — everything stays on your device
- **Not a greeting card generator** — the tone is intimate and human, not cheesy or commercial

---

## The name

**Companion** — someone who sits beside you while you write. Not writing *for* you. Writing *with* you.

The mark is a simple circle with a line through it: a quiet emblem, not a heart, not a speech bubble. Understated. Confident. Like the product itself.
